The Langlois Bridge

The Langlois Bridge was painted by Vincent van Gogh made with oil on canvas in March 1888 during his time in Arles, France. Inspired by Japanese prints, the artwork depicts a peaceful rural scene with a drawbridge spanning a canal, featuring reflections in the water and figures engaged in daily activities.
