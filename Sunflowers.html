Sunflowers

Sunflowers is a series of paintings created with oil on canvas by Vincent van Gogh in January 1889 while he was in Arles, France. These vibrant still-life compositions, featuring sunflowers in various stages of bloom, were intended to decorate the room of his friend Paul Gauguin. Van Gogh used bold yellows and thick, expressive brushstrokes to capture the flowers' warmth and vitality, symbolizing gratitude and friendship.
